# Naturvårdsverkets generella riktvärden

## Source

Naturvårdsverkets rapport 5976: "Riktvärden för förorenad mark – Modellbeskrivning och vägledning"

Published by Naturvårdsverket (Swedish Environmental Protection Agency). Revised 2022.

- Report: https://www.naturvardsverket.se/om-miljoarbetet/miljoovervakning/miljoovervakning/fororenade-omraden/riktvarden-for-fororenad-mark/
- The report defines guideline values for contaminated soil to support risk assessment decisions.

## Categories

- **KM (Känslig Markanvändning)**: Sensitive land use. Applies to residential areas, schools, kindergartens, playgrounds. The most restrictive thresholds.
- **MKM (Mindre Känslig Markanvändning)**: Less sensitive land use. Applies to industrial areas, roads, parking lots. Less restrictive thresholds.

Classification is based on total content (mg/kg TS) in soil.

## 2022 Revision Notes

Key changes in the 2022 revision:
- **Bly MKM**: Lowered from 400 to 180 mg/kg TS. Significant impact on classification of moderately contaminated soils.
- **Kadmium KM**: Lowered from 0.8 to 0.7 mg/kg TS.
- Several other minor adjustments.

## Asphalt-specific Classification

The NV riktvärden are primarily designed for soil (jord). For asphalt classification, the commonly used system is based on PAH-16 total content:
- < 70 mg/kg: Generally clean (can be recycled)
- 70-300 mg/kg: Moderate contamination (restricted reuse)
- > 300 mg/kg: Contaminated (requires special handling)

This asphalt classification is handled separately from the NV riktvärden system and will be implemented in Phase 2.
